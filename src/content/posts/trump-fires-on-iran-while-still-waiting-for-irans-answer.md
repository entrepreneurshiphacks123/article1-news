  The U.S. struck military targets inside Iran after Iranian forces fired on
  three American Navy destroyers in the Strait of Hormuz, Bloomberg's Stuart
  Livingstone-Wallace reports. The exchange marks a sharp escalation in a
  conflict now entering its third month, with no exit secured.


  Here's the operational contradiction: the Trump administration struck Iran and
  simultaneously is waiting for Tehran to accept a U.S. proposal to reopen the
  strait and end the war. You can't signal good-faith negotiation and kinetic
  escalation in the same news cycle without one undermining the other.


  Who benefits from ambiguity dragging on? Not the Navy ships transiting one of
  the world's most commercially critical chokepoints. The administration is
  running a coercive diplomacy play — pressure Iran to the table — but three
  months in, the leverage theory isn't producing a signed deal. That's the
  strategic problem.
