  Tennessee Republicans passed a new congressional map this week that dismantles
  Rep. Steve Cohen's Memphis-anchored district — the state's only majority-Black
  congressional seat and its only Democratic House seat — according to reporting
  by The Hill. The move follows direct pressure from President Trump to redraw
  the map to Republican advantage ahead of November 2026.


  Said Cohen: "Next stop is the courts."


  This is the oldest play in the post-Reconstruction toolkit: crack a
  geographically concentrated minority community across multiple districts to
  dilute its voting strength. The Supreme Court has blocked this maneuver before
  — see Allen v. Milligan (2023) — but each cycle the maps get drawn anyway, the
  litigation drags past the election, and voters live with the unconstitutional
  lines while the case works its way up. Tennessee Republicans appear willing to
  bet the courts are slower than November.
