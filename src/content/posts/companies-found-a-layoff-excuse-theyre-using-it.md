body: >-
  U.S. employers announced 83,387 job cuts in April — a 38 percent increase from
  March — and cited artificial intelligence as the top stated justification for
  the second consecutive month, according to a Thursday report from Challenger,
  Gray & Christmas. That's the strategic tell: AI is becoming the go-to public
  rationale, whether or not it's the operational driver.


  Who benefits from that framing? Executives avoid the political heat of blaming
  tariff-induced demand contraction or borrowing costs. The Trump administration
  avoids accountability for macroeconomic drag. And the AI industry gets to cast
  displacement as inevitability rather than choice.


  For 2026, the question is whether Democrats can separate the AI alibi from the
  underlying policy failures creating the pressure to cut. Workers losing jobs
  to a C-suite press release citing 'AI transformation' are going to feel the
  hit regardless of the label.
