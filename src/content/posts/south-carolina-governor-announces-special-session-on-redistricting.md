  Republican Gov. Henry McMaster is expected to call a special session of the
  South Carolina legislature to redraw the state's congressional map, according
  to CBS News. The move follows a failed procedural vote Tuesday; a special
  session requires only a simple majority, clearing the threshold that blocked
  the bill. Said state Sen. Tom Davis, a Republican who voted against the
  measure: "South Carolina's maps are legally sound, our electoral position is
  strong, and the process being proposed remains constitutionally and
  practically indefensible."
