body: >-
  The Trump administration has deployed what Bloomberg Politics reports (May 7,
  2026) is the largest U.S. troop presence in Latin American history, staging
  forces around the Panama Canal under an explicit stated goal of achieving
  "American dominance" in the region. The move has put governments across Latin
  America on alert.


  The strategic logic is straightforward: treat the Canal as a leverage point —
  against China's port-operating footprint, against Panama's sovereignty claims,
  against any regional actor tempted to test U.S. resolve. The administration is
  betting that raw presence forecloses those options before they develop.


  The problem: military deployments without congressional authorization or
  treaty basis don't project strength so much as expose it. Every regional
  government watching this is now recalculating — not toward deference, but
  toward hedging. That's the opposite of dominance.
