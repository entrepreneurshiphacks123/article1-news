body: >-
  President Trump told NewsNation Friday that he wants a temporary
  Russia-Ukraine ceasefire to grow into something much larger. Said Trump: "I'd
  like to see a big extension" — remarks made after both sides agreed to halt
  fighting through the weekend, a pause that amounts to roughly 72 hours.


  The strategic read is simple: Trump gets a photo-op narrative of progress
  regardless of what happens next. If the ceasefire holds, he claims credit. If
  it collapses, he blames Kyiv or Brussels. The structure of the announcement
  puts the decision leverage entirely in Moscow, not Washington — which is
  exactly where Putin wants it.


  A president with genuine leverage would announce consequences for
  non-extension, not wishes. "Hope" is not a posture; it's a concession.
