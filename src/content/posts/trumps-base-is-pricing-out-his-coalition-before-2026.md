body: >-
  Focus groups conducted by Sarah Longwell and reported in The Bulwark (May 7)
  show measurable defection among Trump's own voters, driven by rising gas
  prices, grocery costs, and the economic fallout from the Iran war — precisely
  the issues Trump ran hardest on in 2024.


  The Longwell data captures something beyond generic disapproval: these are
  voters who bought the 'no more forever wars' and 'I'll lower your costs on day
  one' commitments specifically. Disappointment has curdled into anger. That's a
  different emotional register than buyer's remorse — it's the feeling of having
  been lied to.


  The strategic read: affordability-plus-war is a two-front problem that doesn't
  have a messaging fix. You can't spin a gas pump. If this frustration holds
  into fall 2026 filing season, House Republicans in suburban and exurban
  districts face a base-turnout problem, not just an independent-voter problem —
  and those are two very different campaigns to run.
