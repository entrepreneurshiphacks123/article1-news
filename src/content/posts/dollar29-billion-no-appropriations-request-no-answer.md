    — Article I, Section 9, no money shall be drawn from the Treasury but in
    consequence of appropriations made by law. Secretary Hegseth declined to say

    continuing a pattern that dates at least to the Korean War, when Truman
    committed troops and expenditures faster than Congress was asked to
    authorize them. The difference is that Congress eventually pushed back in
    1951; the open question today is whether it will.
