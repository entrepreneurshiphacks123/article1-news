body: >-
  The NAACP filed an emergency petition Thursday in Davidson County Chancery
  Court seeking to block Tennessee's newly enacted redistricting plan, which
  would eliminate the state's only majority-Black congressional district,
  according to The Hill. The organization argues the plan violates the Tennessee
  state constitution and urged Gov. Bill Lee and the legislature not to enact it
  while litigation proceeds.


  This is the oldest move in the Southern legislative playbook — and its
  longevity is the point. From the White Primary cases of the 1920s through the
  post-Shelby County dismantling of preclearance after 2013, the mechanism has
  been consistent: draw lines that dilute Black political power, then force
  voting rights advocates to exhaust themselves in court while the election
  cycles run. The burden of proof lands on the injured party.


  The state constitutional framing is significant. By grounding the claim in
  Tennessee's own charter rather than solely in federal law, the NAACP may be
  trying to find ground that the current Supreme Court cannot easily reach.
