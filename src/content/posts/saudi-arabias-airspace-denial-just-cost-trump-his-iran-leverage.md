  The New York Times reports that Trump reversed a plan involving the Strait of
  Hormuz after Saudi Arabia refused to grant U.S. forces airspace access — a
  denial that forced a direct operational retreat before any confrontation with
  Iran materialized.


  The strategic damage here is layered. Saudi Arabia is Trump's showcase ally in
  the region — the relationship he has personally invested in and publicly
  claimed as proof that his transactional diplomacy works. A quiet airspace
  refusal doesn't just constrain one operation; it signals to Tehran that the
  coalition behind any U.S. threat has real cracks.


  The Times notes the episode suggests Trump's unpredictable approach to Iran
  has strained ties with Riyadh. That's the operator's read in one sentence:
  when your closest ally won't let you use their sky, your adversary notices.
  Iran is watching what the Saudis just demonstrated — that 'maximum pressure'
  has a ceiling, and it's lower than advertised.
