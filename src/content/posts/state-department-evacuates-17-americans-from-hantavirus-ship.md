body: >-
  The State Department is dispatching a charter flight to retrieve 17 Americans
  stranded aboard the MV Hondius cruise ship after a hantavirus outbreak,
  according to The Hill. The vessel is expected to dock off the coast of Spain
  this weekend.


  The operational details are thin, but the strategic read is straightforward:
  the administration is moving fast enough to avoid a visible consular failure.
  Seventeen citizens, a named pathogen, international waters — the liability
  window is narrow.


  Hantavirus has no approved antiviral treatment and carries a significant
  fatality rate in its pulmonary form. A charter repatriation keeps infected or
  exposed Americans out of foreign hospital systems and inside U.S. public
  health jurisdiction — the right call, and the minimum the State Department's
  evacuation protocols require.
