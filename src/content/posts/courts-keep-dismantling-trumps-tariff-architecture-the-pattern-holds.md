body: >-
  A federal trade court declared Trump's 10% global tariffs unlawful on May 7,
  Bloomberg Politics reports — the second major judicial blow to the
  administration's tariff regime after the Supreme Court vacated earlier levies.
  The ruling strikes at the statutory foundation the White House has used to
  justify sweeping unilateral trade action.


  The Bloomberg summary notes this is a "fresh blow to the administration's
  economic agenda," arriving months after the Supreme Court's prior intervention
  — meaning two separate courts, at two different levels, have now rejected
  Trump's tariff authority on constitutional and statutory grounds.


  The strategic read: every invalidation narrows the legal theories available on
  remand and signals to trading partners that these tariffs carry litigation
  risk. Congress still hasn't moved to ratify or replace the authority. That
  inaction is a choice — and it leaves the executive branch exposed, the markets
  uncertain, and the accountability gap exactly where Trump wants it.
