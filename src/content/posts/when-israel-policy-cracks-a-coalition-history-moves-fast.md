      The last sustained crack in bipartisan Israel consensus came during the
      1956 Suez Crisis, when Eisenhower broke with Israel, Britain, and France
      and forced a withdrawal — to the fury of many in both parties. Before
      that, Harry Truman's 1948 recognition of Israel split his own State
      Department so severely that Secretary George Marshall threatened to
      resign. The through-line: U.S.-Israel policy has always been held together

      strains that consensus — or when a prime minister's conduct makes it
      untenable — the coalition fragments faster than the institutions can
      absorb.
