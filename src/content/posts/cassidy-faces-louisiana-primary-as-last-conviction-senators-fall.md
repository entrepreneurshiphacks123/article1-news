  Louisiana Sen. Bill Cassidy, one of seven Republican senators who voted to
  convict Donald Trump in 2021, faces a closed GOP primary where Trump-endorsed
  Rep. Julia Letlow and former Rep. John Fleming are his opponents, according to
  NBC News. Of the 17 House and Senate Republicans who voted to impeach or
  convict Trump, the only three who survived subsequent elections — Reps. Dan
  Newhouse, David Valadao, and Sen. Lisa Murkowski — ran in open or jungle
  primaries; every pro-impeachment Republican forced into a closed Republican
  primary was either defeated or declined to run again.
