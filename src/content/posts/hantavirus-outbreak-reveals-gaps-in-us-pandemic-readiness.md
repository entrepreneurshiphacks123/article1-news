  A hantavirus outbreak aboard the expedition vessel MV Hondius, which left
  Argentina in April and has killed three passengers, is drawing attention to
  deteriorating U.S. global health infrastructure under the Trump
  administration, according to The Bulwark. Said Jeanne Marrazzo, who leads the
  Infectious Diseases Society of America: "It's probably going to burn itself
  out" — but she and other specialists note that the South African lab that
  identified the strain benefited from years of PEPFAR funding, a program Trump
  hit with a stop-work order after taking office.
