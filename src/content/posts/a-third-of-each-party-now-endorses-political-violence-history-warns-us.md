body: >-
  New polling analyzed by Carroll Doherty, former political research director at
  Pew, finds roughly a third of both Republicans and Democrats now agree that
  'Americans may have to resort to violence in order to get the country back on
  track' — with Democratic agreement more than doubling from 12 percent in March
  2024 to 28 percent by September 2025, per an NPR/PBS/Marist survey. Republican
  support moved from 28 to 31 percent over the same period. A Gallup survey
  found 30 percent of adults under 30 said violence was 'sometimes okay' to
  achieve political aims — roughly eight times the rate of those 60 and older.
  Said University of Chicago professor Robert Pape to the New York Times: 'Once
  we have tens of millions of Americans supporting political violence, this can
  create spirals.' Every previous erosion of anti-violence consensus in American
  history — Reconstruction's collapse, the 1919 Red Scare, the late 1960s —
  preceded, not followed, institutional breakdown. The direction of causation
  matters enormously.
