  Russian President Vladimir Putin held his annual Victory Day parade on
  Moscow's Red Square Saturday — celebrating the Soviet defeat of Nazi Germany —
  as a Trump-announced three-day ceasefire with Ukraine briefly quieted the
  front lines, Bloomberg Politics reports. The Kremlin had imposed intensified
  security measures ahead of the event in response to escalating Ukrainian drone
  attacks.


  Read the play: Putin gets his parade, his symbolism, and a ceasefire he didn't
  negotiate — announced instead by the American president. That sequencing
  matters. Trump absorbs the diplomatic cost of pressuring Ukraine to stand
  down; Putin absorbs the optics win.


  A three-day pause is not a peace process. It's a photo backdrop. The side that
  walks away from this weekend looking like a statesman — and it isn't Zelensky
  — is the side that controlled the calendar.
