  RealClearPolitics reports that the Trump administration is effectively ceding
  management of Ukraine's future to European partners — pulling back American
  ownership of the war's diplomatic endgame and leaving the continent to hold
  whatever ceasefire architecture emerges.


  The strategic read is bleak for Kyiv: Europe is fractious, slow, and still
  dependent on U.S. logistics and intelligence. The question the outlet poses is
  whether a divided European bloc can simultaneously sustain military pressure
  on Russia, negotiate a credible peace, and push democratic reform inside a
  Ukraine still plagued by oligarchic capture.


  Who benefits immediately: Putin, who has consistently bet that Western unity
  dissolves under sustained pressure. Who gets the bill: European taxpayers and
  Ukrainian civilians. Trump walks away from a war he couldn't win and couldn't
  end — and calls it a handoff.
