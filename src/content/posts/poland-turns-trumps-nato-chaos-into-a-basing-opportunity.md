body: >-
  Warsaw is ready to absorb additional U.S. troops if the Trump administration
  follows through on relocating forces from Germany, Poland's defense minister
  said Saturday, per Bloomberg Politics. The offer converts what Trump frames as
  punishment for Berlin into a strategic windfall for the alliance's eastern
  flank — exactly where Russia's threat is most acute.


  Who benefits here: Poland locks in a deeper American military footprint at
  Germany's expense, hardening its deterrence posture without spending a dollar
  on negotiation. Trump gets to look like he's disciplining a NATO free-rider.
  The actual loser is alliance coherence — repositioning forces for political
  theater, not operational logic, degrades the integrated command structure that
  makes the deterrent credible.


  The play Warsaw is running is smart. The play Washington is enabling is not.
