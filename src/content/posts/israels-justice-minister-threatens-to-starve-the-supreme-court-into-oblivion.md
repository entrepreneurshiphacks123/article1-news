body: >-
  Justice Minister Yariv Levin told Ynet this week that if his two preferred
  judicial nominees — conservative academics Aviad Bakshi and Raphael Bitton —
  are not seated on Israel's Supreme Court, he will simply stop filling
  vacancies until the court ceases to function. The math is explicit: four seats
  already vacant, nine total empty by the end of the next Knesset term, out of
  fifteen total. Six justices remaining — a court hollowed to irrelevance.


  Said Levin: "If there is no willingness, the court will gradually disappear on
  its own." On a concurrent deadline from the court to produce a timeline for
  convening the Judicial Selection Committee, his office declined to confirm
  compliance. He has not convened that committee since January 2025.


  The pattern is older than the state: when a minister controls the machinery of
  appointment and refuses to operate it, the constitution doesn't break — it
  drains. Andrew Johnson tried it with Reconstruction. The attrition strategy is
  the quieter cousin of outright defiance, and historically the harder one to
  reverse.
