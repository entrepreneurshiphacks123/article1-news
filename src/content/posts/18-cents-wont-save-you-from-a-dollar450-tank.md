    With the national average above $4.50 a gallon, a full gas-tax suspension
    saves a driver roughly $2.70 on a 15-gallon fill-up. That's the price of a
    candy bar, not meaningful relief. The real play here isn't at the pump —
    it's optics heading into a tough political stretch. What gets buried: the
    federal gas tax funds the Highway Trust Fund, so pausing it doesn't
    eliminate the cost, it shifts it onto the deficit or future infrastructure
    shortfalls. Congress appropriates that revenue; the executive branch
    floating a unilateral pause is a test of how much fiscal authority the White
    House can claim without a floor vote.
