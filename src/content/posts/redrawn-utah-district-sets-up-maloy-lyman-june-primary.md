  Incumbent Rep. Celeste Maloy and former state Rep. Phil Lyman will face off in
  Utah's 3rd Congressional District Republican primary on June 23, under new
  court-ordered boundaries a district judge drew after ruling the 2021 map an
  illegal partisan gerrymander, according to Ballotpedia News. Said University
  of Utah Prof. Jason Hinckley: "This is definitely a competitive race, but it
  is one where Maloy starts from a position of strength" — Maloy narrowly
  defeated Lyman 51% to 49% at the April 25 state GOP convention, short of the
  60% threshold needed to avoid a primary.
