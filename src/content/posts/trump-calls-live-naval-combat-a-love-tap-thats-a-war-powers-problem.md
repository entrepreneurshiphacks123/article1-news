  forces targeted American destroyers and the U.S. responded with strikes on

  the ceasefire remains "intact." That framing isn't spin for Tehran — it's spin
  for Congress. If it's a love tap, it's not hostilities. If it's not
  hostilities, the War Powers Clock doesn't start.


  The play here: hold oil markets steady, avoid a floor vote on authorizing
  force, and keep the conflict legally ambiguous long enough to negotiate or
  escalate on Trump's timeline. Congress should be demanding a legal
  classification in writing — today. The Strait of Hormuz handles roughly 20
  percent of global oil trade. "Love tap" is doing a lot of work.
