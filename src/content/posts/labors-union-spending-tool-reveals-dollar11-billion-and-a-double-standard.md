body: >-
  The Department of Labor has released a new public tracking tool for union
  spending, according to The Hill, covering how $11 billion in annual union dues
  are allocated — including political activities. The move is framed as a
  transparency measure for union members and the general public.


  Here's the strategic read: disclosure requirements applied asymmetrically are
  a political weapon, not a principle. Unions must now expose every dollar to
  public scrutiny. Corporate dark money — the anonymous 501(c)(4) and LLC
  pass-through machinery that Citizens United opened — faces no equivalent
  federal disclosure mandate. That asymmetry doesn't serve democratic
  accountability; it defunds one side of the table.


  The $11 billion figure is real and union members deserve to know how it's
  spent. But transparency that flows only toward labor while corporate political
  spending stays in the shadows isn't reform — it's targeting.
