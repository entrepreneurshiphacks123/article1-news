  With Trump and Xi scheduled to meet May 14–15, the U.S. is scrambling to
  freeze the Gulf crisis before it arrives at the table as a liability,
  according to reporting from The Hill. The strategic logic is simple: every day
  the Iran situation remains unresolved is a day Beijing watches Washington burn
  down its own negotiating position.


  The damage is structural. Saudi Arabia, the anchor of U.S. Gulf influence, is
  diversifying its security relationships — which means China walks into this
  summit knowing it has more running room than it did six months ago.


  The play for Xi: don't negotiate, just wait. A counterparty managing an active
  regional crisis, burning political capital and credibility with traditional
  allies, has less leverage on trade, Taiwan, and technology controls. Trump
  needed a strong hand. He's arriving with a complicated one.
