  NBC News reported Wednesday that the Trump administration abruptly halted its
  naval escort program guiding commercial ships through the Strait of Hormuz —
  reversing course after direct pressure from Saudi Arabia, according to
  correspondents Garrett Haake, Courtney Kube, and Brian Cheung. The walkback
  lands while national gas prices are averaging above $4.50, per NBC's
  reporting.


  Rubio's public spin, per NBC: the operation was a "benevolent action" —
  framing designed to make a retreat look like a choice. It isn't fooling anyone
  who watched the timeline.


  Read the ledger: Riyadh wanted the program dead; it's dead. The White House is
  managing $4.50 gas with no leverage over Iran's residual Hormuz capabilities
  and no durable deal in sight. That's not dealmaking — that's getting played by
  two counterparties simultaneously while American consumers eat the tab at the
  pump.
