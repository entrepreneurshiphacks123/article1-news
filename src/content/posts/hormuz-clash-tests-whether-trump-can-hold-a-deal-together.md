body: >-
  The U.S. and Iran exchanged fire near the Strait of Hormuz on May 8, Bloomberg
  Politics reports, a direct military confrontation that threatens to collapse a
  fragile ceasefire while the two governments are still negotiating a permanent
  end to hostilities. The timing is the tell: escalation arrived as Tehran's
  formal response to a proposed deal was still pending.


  The strategic logic cuts both ways. Iran's hardliners benefit from a collapse
  — no deal means no constraints, and a U.S. flinch under fire hands them a
  propaganda win. But the Trump White House also needs an off-ramp: a failed
  negotiation with live fire in the most critical oil chokepoint on earth is a
  liability, not a trophy.


  Watch which side characterizes the clash as the other's provocation. Whoever
  controls that narrative controls the excuse to walk away — or to demand
  concessions before returning to the table.
