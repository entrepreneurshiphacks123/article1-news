  The United States struck Iranian targets and Iran returned fire in an exchange
  over the Strait of Hormuz, per NYT Politics reporting published May 7, 2026 —
  the latest military escalation in a week the outlet describes as marked by
  "mixed signals in the region." The context matters: NYT's summary states
  explicitly that Trump is "searching for an off-ramp in the war that he
  started."


  That framing is the whole story strategically. A president who initiated
  hostilities now needs a face-saving exit — which means Iran holds significant
  negotiating leverage simply by not escalating further. Every Iranian
  counterstrike that stays below a threshold of mass casualties actually helps
  Tehran: it demonstrates resolve without giving hawks in Washington a casus
  belli for deeper commitment.


  For Congress, the constitutional question is overdue: no authorization for use
  of military force has been cited publicly. The off-ramp search is a White
  House problem; the war powers vacuum is everyone's problem.
