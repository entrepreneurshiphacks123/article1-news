  The Iranian army detained an oil tanker on May 8, Bloomberg Politics reported,
  citing the semi-official Tasnim news agency. Tehran's stated rationale: the
  vessel was "attempting to disrupt oil exports and the interests of the Iranian
  nation."


  That framing is doing a lot of work. Iran casting a seizure as defensive —
  protecting its own exports — is a tell. It's the language of a government
  managing domestic optics while signaling coercive capacity outward,
  simultaneously.


  The play: with U.S.-Iran nuclear talks sputtering and maximum-pressure
  sanctions back on the table, Tehran has a demonstrated incentive to
  demonstrate Strait leverage without crossing a threshold that invites military
  response. Tanker seizures are that instrument — cheap, deniable as
  "violations" enforcement, and loud enough to move insurance markets and
  shipping lanes. Watch whether this is a one-off or the opening move in a
  pressure sequence.
