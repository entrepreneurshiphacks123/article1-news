  U.S. forces struck Iranian missile and drone launch sites near the Strait of
  Hormuz on May 8, targeting assets the Pentagon said were responsible for
  attacking three American warships in transit, according to U.S. Central
  Command via Bloomberg Politics. No vessels were hit. The strike escalates a
  conflict that had been paused — but President Trump simultaneously insisted
  the monthlong ceasefire remains in effect.


  That's the play: hit Iran hard enough to satisfy domestic hawks, then declare
  the ceasefire unbroken so talks toward a permanent deal can continue. Trump
  gets the optics of strength and the optics of diplomacy in the same 24-hour
  window.


  The strategic risk is real. A ceasefire that survives a direct military strike
  on Iranian territory is either an unusually durable framework or a fiction
  both sides are maintaining for their own reasons — and fictions near Hormuz
  tend to end badly for oil markets and worse for the sailors who transit it.
